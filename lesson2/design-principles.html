Rule of Thirds

Medium

medium.com

In medium.com, it is apparent that when we first enter the site, the sentence: "Write, read, and connect" is the first thing that we read and it feels right; this being placed on the "thirds" of the page. It creates that balance in the page and it is more aesthetically pleasing to the eye rather than placing it at the center, then it won't look and feel right and won't look as balanced.

White Space & Clean Design

Shopify

shopify.com

When you click on shopify.com, you don't get overwhelmed with information, you know where to first look and where to focus on; you can digest information easier. The very first sentence we see is "The easiest way to start an online store"; that in itself tells us what shopify.com is all about. Then there is enough white space between that sentence and the prompt to give them your email address. In the overall home page of the website, you can see that white space is apparent and it creates balance in the whole page.

PARC: Contrast

Wistia

wistia.com

When you enter wistia.com, the contrasting colors between the blue, pink, and white are pleasing to the eyes. Our brains tend to notice the differences or contrasts between different elements. In this particular webpage, it is the contrast between the colors and the fonts that make us notice the important elements. In this case, the pink buttons with a contrasting white text and a contrasting blue background makes it stand out; and it can be seen that the contrasts are apparent in the whole page.
